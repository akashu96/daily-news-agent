## 📸 1. MARKET SNAPSHOT — Where Do We Stand Right Now?

Think of this as the morning health check-up for the market.

- 

The **BSE Sensex** is trading around **76,663** (up ~185 points or +0.24%) and the **NSE Nifty 50** is at approximately **23,916** (up ~51 points or +0.21%) as of early trade on July 1, 2026.



- 

However, the indices are trading **below the psychologically important 24,000 mark**, a level they haven't been able to cross convincingly during most of the past two weeks of geopolitics-driven trade.



- 

Tuesday (June 30) ended on a **weak note** — the final trading day of June. The Sensex fell 249.70 points (−0.33%) to close at **76,478.67**, and the Nifty 50 declined 80.50 points (−0.34%) to settle at **23,865.75**.



- 

Interestingly, **broader markets (mid-caps and small-caps) are outperforming** the big indices — the Nifty Smallcap 250 rose 0.72% while benchmarks struggled, showing that investor interest is shifting to smaller, growth-oriented companies.



- 

**Foreign investors (FIIs) are selling** — they offloaded ₹2,556.75 crore worth of shares on June 30. But **domestic investors (DIIs) are buying strongly** with purchases of ₹6,842.34 crore, helping absorb the selling pressure.



> 🟡 **In plain English:** The market is "range-bound" — not crashing, but not flying either. It's like a car stuck in traffic — the engine is running fine, but there's a roadblock (geopolitical uncertainty) ahead.

## 📊 2. KEY TECHNICAL LEVELS & MOMENTUM

*Think of technical levels like floors and ceilings in a building. The market bounces between them.*

### 🏗️ The Key Floors (Support) and Ceilings (Resistance):

| Zone | Level | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| 🔴 Strong Resistance | **24,100–24,200** | "Ceiling" — Bulls must break this to go higher |
| 🟡 Next Target (if broken) | **24,400–24,600** | Next destination if 24,200 is crossed |
| 🟢 Immediate Support | **23,900** | First safety net — a floor below current prices |
| 🔴 Critical Support | **23,800** | If this breaks, more selling could follow |



The **24,100–24,200 region** continues to act as an immediate resistance zone. A sustained breakout above this band would revive bullish (upward) momentum and could pave the way for an advance towards the **24,400** region.





On the downside, **23,900 is the immediate support**, followed by the **23,800 zone**. Holding above this region will be crucial to prevent further weakness.
